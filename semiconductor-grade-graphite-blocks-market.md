<p><p>The semiconductor grade graphite blocks market comprises three main types: extruded graphite, molded graphite, and isostatic graphite. Extruded graphite is formed through a process that enhances density and strength, making it suitable for applications requiring high thermal conductivity. Molded graphite is produced by compacting graphite powder in molds, offering versatile shapes and good mechanical properties. Isostatic graphite is created using a high-pressure process, resulting in uniform density and superior performance in demanding environments, making it ideal for semiconductor manufacturing.</p></p>
