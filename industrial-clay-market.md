<p><p>The industrial clay market encompasses various types, each with unique properties and applications. Kaolin, known for its whiteness and plasticity, is widely used in ceramics and paper. Smectite, characterized by its swelling properties, is vital in drilling fluids and absorbents. Illite, often found in ceramics and agriculture, is known for its stability and nutrient retention. Chlorite, with its lubricating properties, is used in cosmetics and as a filler. Other clays include bentonite and diatomite, serving specialized industrial needs.</p></p>
